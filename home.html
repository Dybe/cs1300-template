Persona & Storyboarding

PS4's DualShock 4 Wireless Controller

The Dualshock 4 Wireless controller is a controller used to control and interact with the PlayStation 4 gaming console. In addition to the buttons, the controller has a headphone jack and a Micro-B charging port. See picture and sketch below for reference

Interface: PS4 Dualshock 4 Wireless controller

Description and Sketch: The Dualshock 4 Wireless controller is a controller used to control and interact with the PlayStation 4 gaming console. In addition to the buttons, the controller has a headphone jack and a Micro-B charging port.

Observations

I observed four users interact with the controllers. My users were males between the ages of 20 - 22. When playing a game, they all held the controller using both hands, generally used their thumbs and two other fingers to press the controller buttons, although some would occasionally engage one more finger if they were trying to do a complex move while playing a game. When users were using the controller to interact with non-game PS4 applications, they sometimes held the controller using one or just pressed a button while the controller was on a flat surface as it generally required less buttons to interact with non-game applications.

Interviews:

I interviewed three users and asked them the following questions:

  • How often do you use the controller?
  • How do you hold it?
  • How long(on average) do you use it per session?
  • What do you like about the controller?
  • What do you not like about the controller?
  • How does this controller compare to others you have used before?
  • When does the controller frustrate you?

These were the insights I gathered:

User 1:

  • He uses the controller to play FIFA with friends
  • He usually uses the controller for about 30 minutes a day
  • He holds the controller using both hands, uses the thumb and index finger to press the buttons while the rest of the fingers assist with the holding.
  • He likes how easy it is to hold the controller, likes the fact that it is wireless and can use if from different angles
  • User finds the controller easier to hold and more comfortable for longer periods of use than the Xbox controller which the user has used before
  • He loves the rubber on the joysticks.
  • He thinks the shape of the controller makes for a good strong grip

User 2:

  • Uses the controller for about 20 minutes per session.
  • Uses the controller to play FIFA and use other PS4 apps such as YouTube and Netflix
  • Does not like it when they have to use the controller to type alphanumeric characters when using some PS4 applications. The longer the phrases they have to type the more pronounced the frustration
  • Holds the controller with both hands. Uses thumbs and index fingers to press the buttons if playing FIFA. Otherwise, just the thumb since they don't have to press multiple buttons at once when using apps.
  • Finds holding the controller comfortable but isn't sure they would not experience discomfort if they used the controller for long periods of time. Think it is easier hold and more comfortable than the Xbox controller

User 3:

  • Uses the controller 10 minutes a day to play FIFA with friends.
  • Thinks it is lighter than others which makes it easy to hold
  • Does not like the positioning of the left JoyStick
  • Prefers the Xbox controller because of the positioning of the joystick

Personas

I came up with the following personas based on my observations and insights from user interviews.

Persona 1 (Intense Ignacio)

Intense Ignacio is a 16-year old rugby player who lives with his Family. He enjoys playing games (FIFA,2K,Madden etc) with his teammates after rugby practice.Ignacio is very competitive and loves to win.  

Ignacio feels that after a few hours, the controller feels uncomfortable and sometimes he blisters his thumbs due to prolonged use.This persona represents users of the DualShock controller whose main hobby is playing video games on the PlayStation. He does so because he has some of the common characteristics of frequent PS4 users.

Ignacio’s empathy map:

Thinks
  • I can go for one more game
  • Why isn’t this controller always fully charged
  • I am the best FIFA player I know

Says
  • “Do you want to play one more game?”
  • “There’s no way you’re gonna beat me”
  • “Are you kidding? I love Fortnite”
  • “Let’s get them bro”
  • “Good game”

Does
  • Drinks an energy drink to remain energized
  • Presses the buttons very fast
  • Celebrates loudly when they beat an opponent
  • Usually uses the playstation for an hour or more in one sitting

Feels
  • Always always motivated to play and compete with other players
  • Anxious that they might lose

Persona 2 (Casual Cassius)

Casual Cassius is a 28 year old professional who lives with two roommates who are also professionals. Cassius enjoys playing games with his roommate on their shared PS4 when he gets the chance. He has non-game apps installed on his PS4 as well and uses them somewhat often.

Sometimes Cassius would realize that a controller’s battery is dead when he wants to use it.

Cassius hates how long it takes him to type long keywords when searching YouTube on the PS4.

Cassius represents users who have the PlayStation relatively casually because they don’t have the time or are not as motivated to play games for long. They have the PS4 because they occasionally crave a game or two against their friends. Some of these users use the PS4 for non-gaming purposes such as Netflix and Youtube even more than they do play games.

Thinks
  • It’s midnight, I should go to bed.
  • A game is not worth playing, If I will be playing alone
  • I am good player, I just don’t play enough

Says

  • “Good game”
  • “I am not that good, I haven’t played in a while”
  • “I can’t play right now, I am tired”
  • “I am busy dude”

Does
  • Downloads Netflix from the PlayStation store and watches his favorite show
  • Makes casual conversation while playing games
  • Loses a game

Feels
  • Frustrated when he loses
  • Excited to play a game with friend(s).
  • More inclined to watch Netflix than play with his roommate sometimes
